The Agile methodology is a way to manage a project by breaking it up into several phases. It involves constant collaboration with stakeholders and continuous improvement at every stage. Once the work begins, teams cycle through a process of planning, executing, and evaluating.

From several variants of agile methodology, we prefer Scrum approch to manage our project development. So, befor starting descusion on this methodology we have to:

  1. Define what scrum is
  2. understand the focus of this approch
  3. principles behind this approch
  4. keyterms used in this approch

  • What is scrum approch?

    • Scrum approch is the set of practice used in agile project managment tha emphesise on daily communication and flexible reassesment of plan that are carried out in a short and ittrative phase of work.The fundamental unit of Scrum is a small team of people, a Scrum Team. The Scrum Team consists of one Scrum Master, one Product Owner, and Developers. Within a Scrum Team, there are no sub-teams or hierarchies. It is a cohesive unit of professionals focused on one objective at a time, the Product Goal.Vist Scrum gaide for more.

  • What is principles behind this approch?

    1. Customer involvment

      2. customer involvment

      In Scrum approch priority is given to the customer and the requirement needed for the development of overall project shall be collected from the customer .

    2. Incremental delivery

      3. incremental delivery

      The software should be developed in incriment with fullfilling the requirements set by the customer at each incriment. The product produced at each encriment is product incriment.

    3. people not process

      4. people not process

      Team memeber should develope the system on their own way with out following prescriptive process.This leads to delivery of good product that ebbrace changes.

    4. Embrace changes

      5. embrace changes

      During and after the developement process requirement shall be changed. So, the system should designed in a way that accomodate these changes.

    5. Maintain simplicity

      6. maintain simplicity

      Scrum approch fellow simplicity in development process of the project as much as posible.

  • keyterms used in process of scrum approch

    1. sprint

      2. Sprint is a cycle of work on which a product of a give incriment is produced. The length of a sprint is 1-4 weaks and it is fixed .

    2. User story

      3. User story means clear discription of of the functionality of the system in term its value to the end user of the product.

    3. Product backlog

      4. product backlog is the collectoin of user story which is priortized by its value and cintineusly updated by product owner to reflect changes.

    4. Sprint planning

      5. It is the plan(it my be finishing some part of the project) facilitated by scrum master to produce a product in agiven sprint .

    5. Daily scrum meeting

      6. Daily scrum meeting is ashort(15 min or less) meeting that happend every working day on which all member of the team report weather they are inprogress or get an obstacle. During this meeting each member of the team exactly report three things to the team and the scrum master makes sure to reach a solution. NOTE: discusion during this meeting not allowed r.

    6. Sprint review

      7. In sprint review the team member demonastrate(but not presantation) what they have done .

    7. Release planning

      8. This is the stage at which the product owner decides the product is ready for relese.

    8. Sprint retrospective

      9. Sprint retrospective is meeting conducted at the end of each sprint .In this meeting review of the process is done by discussing what were wrong in the previous sprint and what will be corrected for the next one .

    9. Scrum Master and Prduct Owner

      10. While the Scrum Master and Product Owner work closely together, these roles are very different. A Scrum Master leads the Agile development team and supports the Product Owner by relaying updates to relevant employees. Product Owners manage the product backlog and ensure the company gains maximum value from the product.

      NOTE: SCRUM MASTER, PRODUCT OWNER AND TEAM ARE PRIMARY ROLES IN SCRUM

    10. Sprint BurnDown Chart

      11. Sprint BurnDown Chart is achart that show the amount of work completed and the total work remained in a given sprint. It is used to track the progress of particular sprint.
